CLEANING DATA WITH SQL

Data cleansing is the process of identifying and correcting inaccurate,
incomplete, or irrelevant data in a database. Inaccurate data can lead to wrong decisions and analysis, incomplete data can lead to missing insights, and irrelevant data can increase noise and decrease efficiency. SQL Server provides powerful tools to cleanse data, including functions to identify and clean up various types of errors, and techniques to standardize and transform data to improve consistency and comparability. Data cleansing is an essential step in data preparation for analysis, reporting, and other data-driven activities. This article will explore the basics of data cleansing with SQL Server, covering common data issues and techniques to solve them.

Predictive analytics examines current and past data patterns to evaluate whether they are likely to reappear. Predictive analytics can also be utilized to boost operational efficiencies and lower risk.
